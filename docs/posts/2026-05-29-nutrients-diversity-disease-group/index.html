Nutrients vs fungal diversity by disease group (Spearman)

pre-specified
alpha-diversity
diet
nutrients
Spearman correlations between dietary nutrients and alpha diversity, overall and stratified by study group (Active IBD, Quiescent, Non-IBD).
Author

IBD Capstone team

Published

May 29, 2026

1 Research question

Are certain nutrients associated with fungal alpha diversity, and do those associations differ across disease groups?

Null (overall): No monotonic association between each nutrient and each alpha diversity metric across all samples.

Null (stratified): Within each study group, no monotonic association between nutrient intake and alpha diversity.

Comparing overall vs group-specific Spearman () is exploratory (small n per group); formal tests of correlation differences were not run.

2 Data

Role Source
Mycobiome (alpha) data/intermediate/alpha_long.rds (make wrangle)
Diet data/processed/dietary_cleaned.xlsx (make dietary on data/raw/OPT_dietary data(ALL).csv)
Disease group Study_group_new on mycobiome metadata (from OPT_MBI sample IDs meta.xlsx)
  • Unit of analysis: Stool sample (22 samples, 12 participants); nutrients are participant means across diet diary days (same as merge_files.R).
  • Join key: Participant_ID = Participant ID (ESHA ID) (normalized OPT codes).
  • Nutrients: Pre-specified panel (fiber, tryptophan, calories, sweeteners, sugars, fats, omegas, vitamin D, folate); see helpers.
  • Inclusion: Samples retained after 02_data_wrangling.R removals.

Coverage: 16 samples, 12 participants, 16 samples with fiber data; study groups: Active IBD, Non-IBD, Quiescent.

3 Methods

Item Choice
Test Spearman rank correlation (cor.test, method = "spearman")
Response Shannon, Simpson, Chao1
Predictors Participant-mean nutrient intake (g, kcal, mg as recorded)
Stratification Study_group_new (Active IBD, Quiescent, Non-IBD) + Overall
Multiple testing Benjamini–Hochberg within each stratum (across nutrient × metric pairs)
Minimum n Correlation only if ≥ 4 complete pairs
Significance α = 0.05
Show code 
spearman_results <- spearman_nutrient_alpha(sample_nutrients)

4 Assumptions and diagnostics

  • Independence: Up to two stool replicates per participant; dietary intake is duplicated across replicates. Exploratory only.
  • Spearman: Monotonic association; no linearity assumption.
  • Stratified n: Few samples per study group limits power; many strata will return NA correlations.

7 Interpretation

Overall Spearman correlations pool all study groups; stratified correlations describe associations within Active IBD, Quiescent, and Non-IBD separately. A nutrient may correlate with diversity overall but not within groups (or vice versa) when group-specific slopes differ or sample sizes are small.

These results do not test whether two correlation coefficients differ significantly between groups; that would require larger n or dedicated methods (e.g. comparing dependent correlations).
